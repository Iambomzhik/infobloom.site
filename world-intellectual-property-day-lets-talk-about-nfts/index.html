World Intellectual Property Day: let’s talk about NFTs

ByInfobloom

May 26, 2022

Non-Fungible Tokens have certified, controlled and protected authorship.

In 2000, the UN established the World Intellectual Property Daythrough the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO), an event that is celebrated every April 26.

This date seeks to promote the importance of intellectual property rights, in order to valuing and safeguarding creativity and innovation of those who produce artistic, literary and scientific works, which may be tangible or intangible.

More precisely, intellectual property is a legal discipline that protects names, symbols, images, among other productions that are used commercially. With this legal guideline, it is possible to prevent any creative work from being plagiarized, since in the event that this happens, the corresponding sanctions will be applied.

The figure of the patents takes relevance in the legal framework of the protection of intellectual property. When a person “patents” his work, he achieves recognition, prestige, authenticity and profit.

Currently in the digital world, a large number of productions circulate on the Internet: a gif, a tweet, an image, among other elements, which are often unique and arise from the exclusive creativity of a person.

However, it can hardly remain on the network without being imitated, plagiarized or intervened. The good news is that although it is difficult to protect these productions, it is not impossible. The NFT’s They came to color the “grays” that exist regarding the intellectual property of digital works.

First, what are NFTs?

NFTs means Non-Fungible Tokens or, what would be the same, a “non-expendable asset”. It is a type of digital asset that cannot be modified and is unique.

For example, a work of art. There may be many copies of a single Picasso painting, however, there is only one that is the original. NFTS work the same way, but in a digital format.

As has already been said, NFTS are digital, this means that they involve non-tangible objects. The inimitable archives can be bought and sold like any other type of property, except that they cannot be “collected” or physically kept like conventional works.

To make it simpler: you can have a work of art that is just a file on your computer and you can’t take it with you. But with a digital certificate that proves that it is our property, its validity and exclusivity is ratified.

Characteristics of NFTs

The first characteristic of Non-Fungible Tokens is that no two are the same (generally, not always) and they are personal and unique. There will be copies, there will be some similar ones, but none exactly the same and there is a certificate -or there should be- that shows who the owner is. an authorship certified, controlled and protected.

Being personal, you cannot buy a license for a work and share it, it does not belong to several people, because the good belongs only to the person who acquires it.

risks

  • Keep in mind that it is a file that “is” on a website and for which, perhaps, thousands of euros have been paid. Now, what happens if that website disappears?
  • By having, for example, a work of art stored in a house, there is a risk that it will be stolen or the house will catch fire. However, the control, broadly speaking, is with the owner.
  • The problem with digital tokens is that users depend on web serversin which they are and what can make them run out of their digital collection, and without money.
  • It does not mean that the risk is always valid, but that it will depend on the company or platform that the person has chosen. It will depend on the site, on the investment.
  • Another of the disadvantages related to NFTs is not related to their security, but to the fingerprint that supposes to operate with the chains of clones, that is to say, with the blockchain.
  • In this sense, the energy consumption that you need to operate with ethereum it can imply a problem because of its carbon “footprint”.
