WARNING – These are the most common scams women fall for

ByInfobloom

May 16, 2022

All people are susceptible to being deceived at some time, when doing business, ordering work or entrusting their money to someone with a specific objective, however, women and the elderly have a greater degree of vulnerability to fall into the clutches of scammers.

They are not one or two women who have lost their savings and their sacrifice following the enchanted flute of people who offer their services to repair household items. This constitutes one of the forms of scams in which women fall the most, in addition to multilevel businesses of which they know very little and informal savings modes such as san.

They are also often the victims of clever scammers who step in to help them in emergency situations and cleverly offer assistance at ATMs.

Laura Rojas urgently needed to repair her washing machine. She heard a man passing by on the street offering his services as an electrical appliance technician. She looked out on the balcony and made him go up to the apartment.

The man checked the equipment and told him that the problem was a damaged piece that cost RD$400.00 and that he could go buy it himself at the hardware store. Confident, she gave him the money and the man left a sack that she assumed were her tools. After almost four hours, Rojas realized that the man would not return and when checking the sack of the supposed tools he noticed with astonishment that it was garbage that “God knows” where he had picked them up.

Fraudsters, in most cases, are mechanics, plumbers, electricians, masons, cabinetmakers, salesmen, home seekers, people who take on any trade and who, with their ability to manipulate, take advantage of the needs of their victims and often their ignorance to steal your money.

Laura Jiménez (not her real name) has been cheated on several times. She denies being trusting, but still she hasn’t managed to avoid being scammed. She says that she took her vehicle to a mechanic for an emergency that arose, the first one she consulted, she told him it was her engine, but she decided to consult another.

The second mechanic told her that the problem was some parts that had to be changed and ruled out the issue of the engine and, given Laura’s doubts, offered her an 18-month guarantee on the work done. After 18 days the engine of the vehicle was damaged and no matter how much he claimed, he could not recover the more than RD$100,000 paid and in the end he decided to sell the vehicle.

Laura affirms that scammers are people who make scams their way of living and that due to the weaknesses of justice, the corruption of repressive agencies and the complexity of judicial processes, they do not pay the consequences of their bad actions.

Another case of deception or fraud was suffered by Maritza Vargas. She narrates that she is looking for a house to rent and that looking in the classifieds of a national newspaper she finds an ad offering a house for rent in Alma Rosa with the requirements with which she was looking for it. She explains that she was contacted by the person responsible for the ad and moved with a co-worker to see the house.

The alleged owner shows her the house and explains that before handing it over to her he must make some arrangements, so she must give him the deposits in order to do so. Ella Maritza tells him that she doesn’t have a lot of money and that she can only advance him RD$2,000. A few days later the man insists that she give him the remaining part and she begins to suspect, when she returns to the place where the house is and asks for the man, it turns out that nobody knows him and that the house was not her property. . Maritza affirms that this type of scam is very common.

experts speak

For journalist Carlos Arturo Guisarre, an expert in economics and personal finance, Multi-level (catalogue sales) businesses are another form of scam in which women are especially vulnerable.

It exposes that unemployment is higher in women and that many of these (single mothers), responsible for family spending, regularly tend to believe in supposed business opportunities offered by scammers, where with a “minimum investment” (RD$10,000) and without many complications they will be able to obtain good profits.

Below, personal finance expert Violeta Saint-Hilaire quotes obehind forms of scams that affect women and how to avoid them.

  • Distribution of assets after a divorce: In divorces, some women tend to lose part of their assets for various reasons, it is possible that they are unaware of the laws and they spend the corresponding time to make the arrangements to receive the part that corresponds to them.
    How to avoid it: Divorce and property distribution are two different actions, which should be carried out at the same time. You have to know which are the rights that correspond according to the regime in which you have decided to divorce.

  • support: It is common that some women do not receive the values ​​that correspond to the ex-spouse to pay them for the maintenance of the children procreated when they were married. Or it may be that the part assigned to her is not enough and the woman has a greater economic burden to support her children when they have custody of them.
    How to avoid it: Make a survey of the children’s expenses and inform the father, if he refuses to pay the part that corresponds to him, consult a lawyer on how to make the request and start the process.

  • romances: Some women, especially the more mature ones, tend to look for partners on the web, they use specialized pages for this type of activity. Their vulnerability begins when they believe that the people contacted are real and that in the future they could establish a serious relationship; To this is added the eloquent words with which this type of virtual Don Juans usually express themselves, they know what they want to hear and they express it that way, once the woman falls into their networks they are able to deliver whatever they ask for . They make him believe that they will come to visit them and that they need help with expenses, or that an emergency has arisen and they are his saviors. The stories are many, how much money has been lost by trusting these scammers.
    How to avoid it: Be cautious when they require contributions of money, especially if you do not know the person you are dealing with.

  • Raffles, gifts. Some women tend to be trusting when they receive some two-for-one product offers, or simply that they have been favored with an expensive gift but that to receive it before they must remit some amount of money. Although these types of scams are common, they continue to fool more and more people.
    How to avoid it: Do not trust sweepstakes or giveaway offers without first checking from a good source that they are real. Nor do you think that to receive a prize you have to contribute money, investigate the veracity of that type of offer before making any money disbursement.
