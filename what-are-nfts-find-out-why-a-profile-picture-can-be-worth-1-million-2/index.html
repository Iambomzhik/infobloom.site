What are NFTs? Find out why a profile picture can be worth $1 million

ByInfobloom

May 10, 2022

Brazilian soccer player Neymar Jr. uses a WhatsApp wallpaper that cost him more than $500,000. The image, an NFT, is a drawing of a pink monkey chewing gum, with a party hat, light blue glasses and an orange background. He also uses the ape as a profile picture on his networks.

Instagram profile of the player Neymar Jr. The profile image used by the player is an NFT that cost him more than $500,000 in January 2022.

Why the heck did that digital image cost so much – you may ask – if anyone can simply search for it on the web, download it in a good size, and manually put it as a WhatsApp background or as a profile picture? The answer to that question explains what an NFT is: because Neymar’s image is unique. It is a unique digital work.

Although it can be copied on the Internet, the information in the digital file is authenticated, it can be said, and only that image is that of the bored monkey number 6633 from the Bored Ape Yacht Club NFT collection.

That’s what an NFT is: a digital asset that has a unique publicly traceable record value. Although there are a thousand copies, only Neymar Jr. owns THE image of the original ape # 6633, which he acquired on January 20. For that ape and another one that he bought, the soccer player paid the equivalent of $1.1 million.

Is all this very new to you? No problem. NFTs are very, very new anyway. Its “boom” is happening now, with an emphasis on the second half of 2021, but with a lot of impact in 2022 (although with a lot of criticism too).

the british dictionary collins chose the word “NFT” as the word of the year 2021. So again it is all.

NFT literally stands for “non-fungible token”, which can be translated to “unique non-fungible object”. Unique digital works. The Urgent Spanish Foundation (Fundéu) proposes to translate it as “unique digital voucher”.

Well, as you may or may not know, thousands of people are involved in what we can call “the world of NFTs”, making million-dollar transactions to acquire these unique digital goods around the world, via the Internet; playing video games that only the owners of some NFTs can access, meeting in digital activities as part of ultra-exclusive clubs of NFT owners, and so on.

Below, we offer you a quick guide to understand what NFTs are, and so that, if you have any questions from your friends or family, simply send them this link.

The boring ape NFT number 6633, owned by Neymar in 2022 (i), and a representation of the Ethereum cryptocurrency (d), the second largest in the world after Bitcoin, and the protagonist in NFT transactions in the world.

The basics: what is an NFT and how were they born?

It is a digital piece (an image, an audio, a gif, etc), that can be certified as unique.

No matter how many copies can be made of the piece, only one is the original. This can be verified through the same technology used to register cryptocurrencies. This technology is called “blockchain”.

“Blockchain” is a digital structure organized in “blocks” or sets of data linked with cryptography, which are progressively added one after another, creating a chain. In cryptocurrencies, these blocks record the valid transactions made with them. In NFTs, these blocks make it possible to record that a digital voucher or non-fungible token is the authentic one and it is possible to check when it was created, how many owners it has had, how much money was paid for it, and who currently owns it (by minus the name of the digital wallet that owns it). This is because NFTs are bought with cryptocurrencies. The NFT is associated with the digital wallet that purchased it.

For example, the boring ape number 6633, which Neymar bought, is linked to the largest store of NFTs in the world, Open Seato the wallet Jan Jay VaultNeymar’s wallet.

The “blockchain” technology works like an accounting book in which the operations are registered and which has independent programmers around the world as verifiers. Millions of verifiers.

It is an independent system, not centralized (unlike banks), and it is a secure system to verify transactions and, in this case, to verify the authenticity of an NFT.

An NFT is basically a way of proclaiming the authenticity of a digital asset or object. The object exists and can be copied to infinity, but the original object is only one. And the owner has the certificate.

This is similar to having an art painting of, say, Picasso. The Picasso painting is accompanied by a certificate of authenticity that shows that it is not a copy. I can have a high quality poster of The Avignon ladiesbut the original painting is in the MoMA, in New York.

Well, an NFT is something like a digital certificate for a digital asset. Little by little, these certificates are provided with added values, beyond just the good itself.

It is increasingly common for owners of authentic NFTs to participate in ultra-exclusive activities, open only to those who have purchased these images. This works by collections. For example, Neymar, snoop-dog, Eminem either Jimmy Fallon, all owners of NFTs from the collection of bored apes, can participate in exclusive virtual activities of this club. For their part, owners of original images from the collection of CryptoPunks, Azuki, Doodles, World of Women, Zombie Club Token or Cool Cats, to name a few, could also have exclusive activities.

Why are NFTs so expensive and who buys them?

It is a matter of supply and demand. As simple as that.

What’s new or weird is that unique digital files like NFTs didn’t exist on the Internet until very recently. Or we had not realized its existence and possible value.

Kevin Roose, technology columnist at the New York Timesexplains that the Internet works essentially like a gigantic copy machine. Any digital file can be infinitely duplicated any number of times. And each copy remains as the original version.

“The Internet’s endless copying quality was great at making digital objects abundant,” notes Roose, “But it was horrible at making them scarce. If I were an artist who wanted to make just 100 first editions of my digital artwork, or a professional athlete who wanted to sell digital trading cards to my fans (and have those trading cards retain value the same way physical trading cards would) , my options were not good”.

NFTs came to change that. Or at least they could, because the truth is that they are still a very niche world.

Many people realized that blockchains could be used to create unique, uncopyable digital files, adds Roose. And because these files were simply entries in a public database, anyone could verify who owned them or track them as they changed hands.

“They are a way of having traceability, transparency and transfer of value, since everything remains in the ‘blockchain’, a digital book that is tamper-proof and maintains a permanent record”, comments the Mexican programmer and crypto-artist Luis Ramírez, to Forbes.

The NFT movement was born on the Ethereum blockchain, the second largest cryptocurrency in the world after Bitcoin. The most important market for digital art or NFTs is currently being developed on the Ethereum “blockchain”.

Online marketplaces like OpenSea or rare they are growing platforms where one can buy NFTs, but they can also be created. The digital files that can be transformed into NFTs are: mp3, mp4, png, jpg and gif.

Some platforms charge an initial fee, and then the creation of each NFT is free. For example, the largest network, OpenSea, which is paid for in Ether, has an initial entry fee that ranges from the equivalent of $20 to $130.

Then, the profits or losses that we can make in the NFT world will respond exclusively to supply and demand. And to speculation, of course.

It is by no means certain that the image of the boring ape number 6633 that Neymar bought for $517,000 will increase in price in three years or three months. There is no certainty. In fact, Gary Vaynerchuk, an entrepreneurial Belarusian-American tycoon enthusiastic about NFTs, recently predicted that about 98% of current NFTs will lose their value.

There are who have earned millions of dollars in a short time with NFTs, but there are also those who they have lost a lot of money.

The dark side of NFTs

Here we will not go into the debate of whether a gif or a jpg file is art or not. After all, if there is demand and there is supply, two worlds come together. Furthermore, it is a matter of significant value.

The French sociologist, philosopher, and cultural theorist Jean Baudrillard explained that an object has a combination of four values: exchange value, functional value, symbolic value, and significant value. What is more valuable to someone? The expensive, the useful, the significant/important, or the famous/celebrated?

“Depends,” digital creator Pak Murat – who sold the most expensive NFT of all time – wrote on Twitter. for more than $91 million, Last December-. “If you want to sell, what is expensive is more valuable. If you want to use it, the useful is more valuable. If you want to save it, what is significant/important is more valuable. If you want to teach it, what is famous/famous is more valuable”.

Every object has a combination of those four values; NFTs included. For this reason, we will not enter into the debate about whether it is art or not, why a gif can cost more than an apartment or why a original meme it can cost the same as a Picasso.

Now, in the NFT world, just like in the world of traditional art or just in the physical world, there are also scams and there is a place for money laundering.

It is not uncommon for an NFT project developer to run away with buyers’ money. Last October, an NFT project called Evolved Apes suddenly disappeared. taking approximately $2.7 million. It was all a scam and a promise of a project with a video game included that never appeared.

Added to this danger, the anonymity of transactions with cryptocurrencies in the “blockchain” -we know the name of the digital wallet, but not the people behind it- allows “dirty” money, coming from illicit activities such as drug trafficking or others, whether it is invested (laundered) in NFTs or in the same cryptocurrencies. This is something very pointed out in the world of “crypto”, and therefore it is in the NFT world.

Likewise, there is also environmental damage linked to NFTs (and cryptocurrencies). The Ethereum blockchain requires excessive energy expenditures for the creation of NFTs. Many times crypto mining centers are buildings full of computers working non-stop, or lineups of computers located in countries or regions where electricity is cheaper. Renewable energy sources are not usually used for these activities.

Finally, we can also mention some details that are at least rare or suspicious in the world of NFTs. Creative director, concept artist and programmer Ryder Ripps, ex-partner of rapper Azealia Banks, posted a thread on Twitter detailing pro-Nazi or neo-Nazi aspects in the most popular NFT collection of all, that of the Yacht Club of Bored Apes.
