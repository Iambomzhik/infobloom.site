Instagram Scams – How to Stay Safe

ByInfobloom

May 18, 2022

Instagram scams have become overwhelmingly popular among cybercriminals. Social media has made it easy for strangers to gain your trust by impersonating people or brands. Sometimes these scammers spend weeks talking to you before pulling off their scam. As this risk increases, you’ll need to know the signs of a scam to avoid having your money or identity stolen.

As our digital lives continue to grow, online scams have progressed to become more deceptive. Phishing on Instagram is just one of many credible strategies you can fall victim to.

Therefore, learning how to identify an Instagram scam is essential for all users of the platform.

What are Instagram scams?

Instagram scammers use various methods to make you a victim of theft or attack. Typically, these cyber scams are after something valuable, like money or confidential information. Some may even try to use it as a means to spread malicious code to your loved ones or co-workers.

In either case, you will find yourself making a decision that could affect you and those around you. The problem with scammers is that it’s not always easy to stay sane. Effective scams tend to prey on your emotions and pretend to be trustworthy. The best scammers pay close attention to detail and make it hard to question their authenticity.

Your best defense is to be aware of the most popular schemes on Instagram.

Risks of identity theft on Instagram

Phishing on Instagram is the most common way scammers hijack your identity and personal accounts. Phishing is a message that urges or persuades you to take action, typically by requesting money or account access details.

Spoofers pose as a trusted person or brand you may know, and the illusion of authenticity can be enough to trick you into giving the hacker what they want.

Instagram has identified the following scams:

  • scammers romantics they ask you for money after gaining your trust, usually through flirtation or seduction.
  • The scammers of lottery they claim that you have won something and ask for a fee to give you the reward.
  • The fraud labor offers to help you earn money if you provide bank information for direct payment.
  • The stratagems of loan they claim to allow you to borrow money instantly for an up-front fee.
  • The scams of false investments They lead you to make a small investment with the intention of increasing your money. Money speculation scams are especially relevant on the platform.
  • Paid Subscription Fraud It tricks you into paying for discounted access to services like music or movie streaming.

Other specific scams could also be a threat to you:

  • The false sponsors of influencers they try to trick growing instagrammers out of their bank details by pretending to be a real brand offering an advertising deal.
  • Instagram Phishing Email Scams they may encourage you to use a fake login link to appeal a terms of service warning on your account. Other emails claim that suspicious activity has occurred and will ask you to register and verify.
  • the draws they ask you to give personal information, make a payment or enter a site in exchange for a prize.

As you can see, identity theft is all about gaining your trust or playing on your emotions to trick you. If you’re not careful, you could be scared or forced to become a victim.

It is worth remembering that scammers are not always quick in their plots, they can take from days to months before starting the scam.

Over time, any Instagram fraud scheme has some kind of “order”. This is the biggest red flag to watch out for. If a brand or person asks you to do something that could put you in danger, it can make you a victim of a scam.

Did you get scammed on Instagram? What should you do next?

If you’ve fallen for an Instagram scam, or think you may be a victim, you need to take steps to reduce the damage.

This is because the personal information you have provided could be used to:

  • steal your identity
  • Empty your bank accounts
  • Violate any of your online accounts
  • ruin your credit
  • Spread malware to people you know

Armed with this information, you can act quickly to put the hacker out of your life for the long haul. You should also be able to undo some or all of the immediate problems they have caused you.

Here are some tips for taking action:

Check your bank accounts and credit cards.Search all your bank activity and statements for anything you don’t remember doing. A scammer who has your financial information can make transfers or purchases from your accounts. Don’t just look for big purchases, cybercriminals can reduce your expenses with small splurges. On occasion, purchases of as little as a few pesos have been discovered. If you have discovered something strange, report it to your bank or credit institution to stop any further use.

Change your passwords. Obviously, you’ll want to change your Instagram password to be safe. But you may have compromised other accounts if you used a fake login form. If you’ve ever reused passwords and usernames, a scammer can break into more of your accounts. Be sure to change passwords in all essential places, like online banking, social media, email, and anywhere you pay bills or shop online.

Use unique passwords for each online account. In addition to changing your passwords, make sure each one is complex and different. If it’s easy for you to remember, it can be easy for a hacker to guess. Passphrases with multiple character types will help keep you secure. Random character strings are the best option. To remember all these new passwords, use an online repository like Kaspersky Password Manager.

Check your credit and freeze it. Scammers have been known to set up new bank accounts and incur all kinds of debt in your name. If your identity has been stolen, it’s wise to check your credit score and report at three of the major institutions that offer these services. Experion, Equifax, and TransUnion collect information differently, so you’ll want to check all of them. Freezing your credit is another vital step in limiting problems caused by known identity theft.

Use an identity theft verification service. Services like these will monitor any suspicious activity on your credit, tax returns, investments, and more. To keep an eye out for any further data breaches, Kaspersky Security Cloud offers constant monitoring and tips to protect you.

How to protect yourself against scams on Instagram

It seems that phishing on Instagram is here to stay, so you have to be prepared. Here are some reliable ways to detect and avoid becoming a victim of fraud.

The best ways to avoid Instagram fraud

If a message asks you to “give” something, be careful. Fraud often involves bank accounts, the sale of something, or the request for other personal information. This could include entering an account or being asked to make a payment for something. If you have received any direct messages or emails that seem suspicious to you, proceed with caution.

Enable two-factor authentication allows you to have one more point of control for account logins, apart from your password. This checkpoint requires the person signing in to verify with another account or device that you have. The types of 2FA offered can include text, email, in-app prompts, biometrics, or even a physical USB stick.

Never use an access link through a message. Always use an official URL and log in directly to your accounts. This is especially important for Instagram phishing email fraud. Scammers are betting that you will react to the urgency in their messages and click to log in without thinking rationally. Always pause and look for the real URL. If there is a real problem, an authentic login will clear it up. You can also follow up with an official support email, chat, or phone line to confirm.

If it sounds too good to be true… it probably is. Sweepstakes, job, and other scams lure you into money to cloud your judgment. Fortunately, you can assume that these offers are rarely real. If you are curious, you can contact the brands directly through the official channels. Search the web for their real website and contact them to ask about the details of the offer.

Always search for official accounts before replying to messages. You may be able to spot authentic brands and people by looking for verified accounts (the ones with a blue checkbox next to their username). The big brands will have links to all the official social networks from their official website. If there is no link to Instagram on their website, it may mean that the brand does not have an official Instagram account. Check the sender’s profile for suspicious traits, such as posts with too many links or hashtags.

If a stranger follows you, be suspicious. Many scams involve gaining your trust by establishing a relationship with you. It is wise to block unknown followers or those who do not share any mutual followers with you. It may be best to set your account to private and only allow people you know to follow you.

If someone you know sends a strange message, contact them directly. Contact the person offline with a phone call or talk to them in person. Ask him if he sent you the message. A scammer may be impersonating them and targeting you and others you know. They may have to report the account and warn the people around them.

Use an antivirus program on all your devices. You’ll want to protect yourself from any malware that might sneak in if you’ve clicked on a suspicious link. Software like Kaspersky Internet Security offers protection against viruses, ransomware, and even helps block phishing.
