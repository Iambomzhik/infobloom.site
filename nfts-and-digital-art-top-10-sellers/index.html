NFTs and digital art: top 10 sellers

ByInfobloom

May 13, 2022

In 2021, NFTs have revolutionized the world of digital art and blockchain gaming, but despite their adoption in these markets, they still have a lot of potential for building Web 3.0. In this article you will learn what NFTs are, what their potential is and which are the 10 best-selling NTFs in the context of digital art.

NFTs and digital art: top 10 sellers

What are NFTs?

NFTs are certificates of ownership and authenticity of physical and digital goods. The acronym NFT stands for Non-Fungible Token, which translates as non-fungible token. NFTs are tokenized assets that are created through smart contracts, assigning ownership to a single owner.

This ownership feature is handled through a unique ID that cannot be replicated. When an NFT is created, a code stored in a smart contract that conforms to the standards of each blockchain is executed. The main characteristics of NFTs is that they are unique, indivisible and transferable.

NFT explosion

The rise of NFTs emerged in 2021, after the radical change in human behavior due to the pandemic. Many people spent more time glued to the internet and to screens and, as a result, digital life took power and is now more valued than in previous years. Cryptocurrencies and NFTs exploded as many more people explored these new digital goods.

Fundamental blocks of Web 3.0

Many layer 1 blockchains deployed their projects between the years 2019 and 2020 focusing on creating an infrastructure for decentralized finance. Due to this, 2020 saw a boom in the DeFi market based on the new infrastructure of these blockchains.

This boom stemmed from the success of Ethereum and its smart contract ecosystem. Many developers saw the potential of decentralized finance and this led to a consolidation of many platforms such as Uniswap, MakerDao, and Aave.

This led to a growth in NFTs as new digital goods, as they not only have the potential to establish ownership of digital goods but physical goods as well. From the growth of DeFi, new safe and accessible markets were created for users. NFTs give way to data ownership that moves towards users and does not get stuck in centralized companies.

In Web 3.0, cryptocurrencies and NFTs are the digital goods of this new economy, layer 1 blockchains are the ecosystems that feed it, DeFi is the new financial system, and DAOs are the institutions that govern the entire ecosystem.

various applications

NFTs can have applications from collectible items, lottery tickets, concert seats, digital art, real estate (both physical and digital), video game objects, credentials, internet domains, and any digital or physical good.

2021 saw a massive increase in NFT trading volume exceeding $23 billion. For now, the applications of NFTs are concentrated in digital art and blockchain games. However, 2022 will bring with it new Web 3.0 platforms with NFTs as protagonists.

Why did they have such growth in 2021?

NFTs had a monumental growth in 2021 due to the fact that the different blockchains were consolidated as more reliable, efficient and secure investment instruments for users. As there was more trust in this new technology, a wider public accessed the platforms where NFTs were transacted.

Growth on a par with cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies and NFTs ceased to be digital goods that only experts, computer scientists and crypto enthusiasts had access to and entered the mainstream world.

With reliable and secure platforms, NFTs could be adopted and used in transactions by anyone on platforms like OpenSea and Rarible.

Looking at the huge growth of cryptocurrencies, many people believed that they had missed out on investing, but found new possibilities in NFTs.

They made their way into digital art

Seeing a new life in NFTs and their great growth in the different networks, many collections of NFTs were displayed as digital art.

Accompanied by massive hype from artists like Beeple, Snoop Dogg, Grimes, Paris Hilton, Eminem, Jimmy Fallon, Logan Paul, and companies like Visa, NFTs grew unprecedentedly in 2021.

Hand in hand with Web 3.0

The Web 3.0 discourse was born at the end of 2017 and the beginning of 2018, but the concept remained only among experts on the subject. A new Web 3.0 discourse emerged in 2021 and took NFTs as one of the fundamental blocks of this new internet revolution.

Investors like Chris Dixon and Naval Ravikant back Web 3.0 and include NFTs as the pieces of property needed to build this new Web.

Top 10 best selling NFTs

1. Merge

  • Sale price: USD$91.8M
  • Sale date: December 2, 2021

Merge is a project from digital artist Pak that explores dynamically generated NFTs. Users could buy quantities of dough, which at the end of the auction generated a unique NFT depending on the purchased dough. The work was acquired by a group of 28,000 collectors through the Nifty Gateway platform.

2. Everydays: The First 5000 Days

  • Sale price: USD$69.3 M
  • Sale date: March 11, 2021

This work created by the artist beeple was sold for 69.3 million dollars at Christie’s auction house, being the most expensive NFT to be sold to a single owner. Beeple’s work is a collage of 5,000 digital illustrations created every day since 2007. The work shows the artist’s progress over more than 13 years.

3.Human One

  • Sale price: USD$28.9M
  • Sale date: November 9, 2021

Human One is another work created by beeple that was sold for 28.9 million dollars at Christie’s auction house. The work is a life size sculpture described as a kinetic video sculpture created with 4 screens.

4.CryptoPunk #7523

  • Sale price: USD$11.8M
  • Sale date: June 10, 2021

CryptoPunks are a collection of NFTs created by Larva Labs known to be one of the most expensive NFT collections on the market. His collection of 10,000 algorithmically generated digital characters was one of the first to be created on the Ethereum network. Each CryptoPunk has unique characteristics which determine its price. In this case, CryptoPunk #7523 sold for $11.8 million at a Sotheby’s Digital Works auction.

5.CryptoPunk #3100

  • Sale price: USD$7.67 M
  • Sale date: March 11, 2021

The CryptoPunk #3100 generated interest thanks to its unique features, such as its blue and white colored ribbon. This NFT was sold on the OpenSea marketplace for $7.67 million.

6.CryptoPunk #7804

  • Sale price: USD$7.56M
  • Sale date: March 10, 2021

This CryptoPunk #7804 was purchased by Dylan Field, CEO of the Figma design company for $7.56 million. He is one of only 9 aliens in the collection.

7. Right-click and Save As guy

  • Sale price: USD$7.09M
  • Sale date: December 9, 2021

NFTs artist XCopy sold his digital artwork Right-click and Save As guy for $7.09 million. The work was bought by “Cozomo_de_Medici”, Snoop Dogg’s alias in the NFT market.

8. Art Blocks: Ringers #109

  • Sale price: USD$6.93M
  • Sale date: October 2, 2021

Art Blocks is a randomly generated NFT project that gives the buyer a unique artwork generated upon purchase. The end result can be a static image, a 3D model, or an interactive experience. Of the collection of 99,000 NFTs, Ringers #109, by Dmitri Cherniak, sold for $6.93 million.

9. CROSS ROAD

  • Sale price: USD$6.6M
  • Sale date: February 25, 2021

CROSSROAD is a beeple play that sold for $6.6 million at Nifty Gateway. The play is a 10-second short film set in a dystopian world where people are seen walking. In the background lies face down a large nude body marked with various insults representing Trump.

10. OCEAN FRONT

  • Sale price: USD$6M
  • Sale date: March 22, 2021

OCEAN FRONT is another beeple work that sold for $6 million at Nifty Gateway. The work presents a structure made of containers forming a building in the middle of the ocean.

Where are NFTs going?

The different projects of NFTs, whether they are digital art or blockchain games, are the beginning of a new chapter in the world of blockchains and cryptocurrencies. As their adoption expands, so do their potential cases.

For now, collectibles, contributions to the metaverse, and real estate may be the short-term sectors where NFTs are implemented. However, their possibilities are broader, since these can represent unique records of ownership.

NFTs provide a layer of truth to the blockchain and it is possible to deliver individualized value using decentralized technology. NFT technology will reshape the structure in which societies function by simplifying the provability of the unique in an open and transparent system.

All this will be possible thanks to the construction of Web 3.0 with the help of cryptocurrencies, NFTs, decentralized finance and DAOs.
